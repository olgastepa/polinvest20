Banking Security Receipt(SKR)

A receipt from the place of safe custody, selected by the owner of the assets to place his asset in the care of an agent (with the conclusion of a fiduciary right), which, as a rule, is a bank or financial institution and is issued as confirmation of the presence of an asset from the fiduciary trustee responsible for the storage of this asset. The owner of the assets may order that such a receipt be sent to a third party. These services usually require payment.

Completely different assets can be placed for safekeeping, such as: stocks, bonds, real estate documents, title deeds, precious or rare-earth metals, explored reserves of oil and natural resources, etc. The owner of SKR can monetize (non-repayable loan) this tool or attract financing for it, like SBLC, Bond or BG and use these funds as an alternative source of financing for projects.
